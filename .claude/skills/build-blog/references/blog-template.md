# Blog Template  - your community blog Style

This template is derived from your product team blog patterns. Adapt the structure to fit the blog type. Not every post needs every section.

---

```markdown
# <Title  - Clear, Specific, Under 80 Characters>

<Author Name>
your company
<Month Day, Year>

<Subtitle or one-line summary of the post>

## Introduction / Hook

<1-2 paragraphs setting up the problem or announcing the news. Lead with why it matters to the reader. No "In this blog post, we'll explore..." openings.>

## <Capability / Section 1  - Descriptive Header>

<2-4 paragraphs explaining the first key capability or topic. Walk through what the user sees and does. Include specific numbers or scale details where available.>

<Optional: customer quote as blockquote>

> "<Quote text>"
>  - <Name> | <Role> | <Company>

## <Capability / Section 2  - Descriptive Header>

<Same pattern. Mix prose with numbered lists when listing related sub-capabilities. Use numbered lists for scanability when covering 3+ items.>

## <Capability / Section 3  - Descriptive Header>

<Continue as needed. Vary section depth. Not every section needs to be the same length.>

## Get Started Today

<Short paragraph pointing readers to action. Keep it direct.>

<Numbered or bulleted list of key links:>
1. <Action>  - <URL or description>
2. <Action>  - <URL or description>
3. <Action>  - <URL or description>

<Optional: Note preview/GA status, version, and any prerequisites.>

---

Updated <Month Day, Year>
Version <X.0>
```

---

## Template Variations

### Feature Announcement (short, ~800 words)
- Hook: What's new and why it matters (1 paragraph)
- 2-3 capability sections (2 paragraphs each)
- Get Started section with links

### Product Deep-Dive (medium, ~1500 words)
- Problem setup (2 paragraphs)
- 3-5 capability sections with screenshots/diagrams
- Customer quote integration
- "About [Product]" boilerplate section
- Get Started with docs links

### Thought Leadership (long, ~2500 words)
- Problem framing with market context (3 paragraphs)
- 4-6 sections mixing analysis with product capabilities
- Multiple customer quotes
- Forward-looking section ("What's next")
- Resources and further reading

---

## Blog Checklist

Before finalizing, verify:
- [ ] Title is under 80 characters and says what the reader gets
- [ ] Opening paragraph hooks with the problem or announcement, not a generic intro
- [ ] Every section has a descriptive header (not "Section 1")
- [ ] Specific numbers used where available (scale, performance, time savings)
- [ ] Customer quotes have attribution (name, role, company)
- [ ] Call to action at the end with working links
- [ ] Preview/GA status noted if applicable
- [ ] Humanized Writing Standard followed (no banned words, no em dashes, varied structure)
- [ ] No "In this blog, we'll explore..." or "In conclusion, we've seen..." patterns
- [ ] Word count matches the agreed length target
